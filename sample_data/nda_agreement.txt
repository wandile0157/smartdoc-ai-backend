WHEREAS the Disclosing Party possesses certain proprietary and confidential information related to artificial intelligence technology, machine learning algorithms, and business strategies;

WHEREAS the Receiving Party desires access to such confidential information for the purpose of evaluating a potential business partnership;

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all information disclosed by the Disclosing Party including but not limited to:
    - Technical data, source code, algorithms, and software
    - Business plans, financial information, and pricing strategies
    - Customer lists, supplier information, and market research
    - Trade secrets, know-how, and proprietary processes
    - Product development plans and research findings
    - Any information marked as "Confidential" or "Proprietary"

1.2 Confidential Information excludes information that:
    - Is publicly available through no breach of this agreement
    - Was rightfully possessed prior to disclosure
    - Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
    - Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction

2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:

2.1 Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
2.2 Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without written consent
2.3 Use Confidential Information solely for the evaluation purpose stated above
2.4 Protect Confidential Information with same care as own confidential information (minimum reasonable care)
2.5 Limit access to employees with legitimate need-to-know who have signed confidentiality agreements
2.6 Not copy, reproduce, or reverse engineer any Confidential Information
2.7 Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon request or termination

3. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

3.1 Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, court order, or government authority, provided:
    - Disclosing Party receives prompt written notice
    - Receiving Party cooperates in seeking protective order
    - Only minimum required information is disclosed

4. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 This agreement shall commence on 10 November 2024 and continue for 3 years.
4.2 Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for 5 years.
4.3 Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice.
4.4 Upon termination, Receiving Party must:
    - Return or destroy all Confidential Information within 7 days
    - Provide written certification of compliance
    - Cease all use of Confidential Information

5. OWNERSHIP AND NO LICENSE

5.1 All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of Disclosing Party.
5.2 No license, rights, or title to intellectual property is granted.
5.3 This agreement does not obligate either party to proceed with any transaction.

6. NO WARRANTIES

6.1 Confidential Information is provided "as is" without warranty of any kind.
6.2 Disclosing Party makes no representations regarding accuracy or completeness.

7. REMEDIES AND ENFORCEMENT

7.1 Receiving Party acknowledges that breach would cause irreparable harm.
7.2 Disclosing Party entitled to injunctive relief without proving damages.
7.3 Receiving Party liable for all damages, costs, and attorney fees resulting from breach.
7.4 Remedies are cumulative and not exclusive.

8. INDEMNIFICATION

Receiving Party shall indemnify and hold harmless Disclosing Party from any loss, damage, liability, or expense arising from:
- Breach of this agreement
- Unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information
- Claims by third parties resulting from Receiving Party's actions

9. NON-SOLICITATION

9.1 For 12 months following termination, Receiving Party shall not:
    - Solicit or hire employees of Disclosing Party
    - Encourage employees to terminate employment
    - Interfere with business relationships

9.2 This clause does not prevent general recruitment advertising.

10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

10.1 Any disputes shall first be addressed through good faith negotiation.
10.2 Unresolved disputes shall be submitted to arbitration under AFSA rules in Johannesburg.
10.3 Arbitration decision shall be final and binding.
10.4 This agreement is governed by the laws of South Africa.
10.5 Jurisdiction: High Court of South Africa, Gauteng Division, Pretoria.

11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 This agreement constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior understandings.
11.2 Amendments must be in writing signed by both parties.
11.3 Waiver of one breach does not waive subsequent breaches.
11.4 If any provision is invalid, remaining provisions remain in full force.
11.5 Neither party may assign this agreement without written consent.
11.6 Notices must be in writing to addresses above or email as confirmed.
11.7 This agreement may be executed in counterparts.

12. ADDITIONAL OBLIGATIONS

12.1 Receiving Party shall implement appropriate security measures including:
    - Secure storage of documents (physical and electronic)
    - Password protection and encryption
    - Access controls and monitoring
    - Regular security audits

12.2 Any breach must be reported to Disclosing Party within 24 hours of discovery.

12.3 Receiving Party shall maintain records of all persons accessing Confidential Information.

13. SPECIFIC PENALTIES

13.1 Unauthorized disclosure: Penalty of R 500,000.00 per incident plus actual damages.
13.2 Each day of continued breach constitutes separate violation.
13.3 Penalties do not limit other remedies available at law or equity.
